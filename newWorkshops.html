What we work on

More than technique.
A way of seeing.

Every session is different, but these are the core things I help every photographer develop — regardless of skill level.

Reading the street

Learning to observe before you raise the camera — light, movement, energy, and what's about to happen next.

Common questions

Before you book.

Do I need photography experience to join?+
Not at all. Both private and group workshops welcome all skill levels — from first-time camera holders to working professionals. A mixed group makes the experience richer. If you're new, I'll help you understand the essentials before we head out on the first morning.
What camera should I bring?+
Any camera works — what matters is how you see. Once you've booked, I'll share a gear checklist tailored to your level and the specific destinations. I shoot on a Leica M11-P, but the camera is the last thing that determines a strong image.
How physical is the workshop?+
Most workshops are rated easy to moderate. We walk for extended periods — typically 3–4 hours per session — but at a relaxed, observation-focused pace. Ladakh involves high altitude with acclimatisation days built in. I always adjust to the group's needs.
What's the group size for group workshops?+
Maximum 5–6 participants depending on the workshop. Small groups mean genuine individual attention on the street, and the ability to move quickly and quietly through the environments we're photographing — which matters a great deal for the kind of work I lead.
Do I need a visa for India?+
Most nationalities can apply for an Indian e-Visa online before arrival — straightforward and usually processed within a few days. Before any trip you'll receive a full briefing document with visa guidance. Check requirements here →
Can I cancel if my plans change?+
Please read the full Terms & Conditions before booking. A deposit secures your place with clear terms around cancellations and transfers. Get in touch and I'm happy to walk you through the policy directly before you commit.
