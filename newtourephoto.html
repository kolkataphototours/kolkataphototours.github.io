Kolkata · My home · My streets

Kolkata — a paradise
for street photographers.

I was raised here. I've been photographing these streets for over a decade — not visiting them, living them. That difference shows in every tour I lead. I know not just where to go, but when the light hits the alley behind the metal market at exactly the right angle. I know which vendor at the flower market will let you in before sunrise. I know where the wrestlers train by the Howrah Bridge and how to approach them with respect.

Kolkata is layered, unpredictable, and endlessly alive. It rewards patience and presence — two things I'll help you develop from the first morning walk.

Where I take you

Kolkata, location by location.

Every tour is customised, but these are the spots I know most intimately — and return to again and again.

Kumartuli

At Kumartuli, clay transforms into divinity. Narrow lanes filled with artisans, textures, and unfinished expressions make this a deeply intimate storytelling space before the festival begins.

Koley Vegetable Market

Chaos finds its rhythm at Koley Market. Layers of colour, motion, and human exchange unfold rapidly—perfect for practising anticipation and working within dense, living frames.

Local Train Ride

The Kolkata local trains are more than transport—they are moving narratives. From fleeting gestures at station stops to layered compositions inside compartments, every second demands awareness.

Colonial Calcutta Streets

In the old quarters of Kolkata, time lingers in fading facades and colonial architecture. Light, shadow, and solitary figures create frames that feel both cinematic and timeless.

Ganga Ghat

Along the banks of the Hooghly River Ghats, life flows quietly. Rituals, reflections, and soft morning light offer moments of stillness within the city's chaos.

North Kolkata Lanes

North Kolkata is where stories live in layers—crumbling mansions, intimate alleys, and everyday life unfolding slowly. It's about patience, observation, and discovering poetry in the ordinary.

Howrah Bridge

At Howrah Bridge, the city pulses at full intensity. Endless human movement, shifting light, and scale come together to challenge both timing and composition.

Wrestlers of Kolkata

In traditional akharas, the wrestlers embody discipline and heritage. The interplay of sweat, light, and raw emotion offers powerful, intimate storytelling opportunities.

Fish and Meat Market

This is where Kolkata's raw energy is most unfiltered. Texture, movement, and human interaction collide—pushing you to work instinctively and embrace discomfort in your frames.

Flower Market

Beneath the bridge, the Mullick Ghat Flower Market bursts with colour and energy. It's a playground for layers, repetition, and decisive moments in tight spaces.

College Street

At College Street, knowledge and nostalgia coexist. Bookstalls, students, and quiet conversations create layered scenes rich with character and depth.

Durga Puja

During Durga Puja, the city transforms into a living canvas. Emotion, devotion, and spectacle come together offering endless moments that demand both sensitivity and timing.
